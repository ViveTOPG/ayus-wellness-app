Our Indian Vedic heritage

How this knowledge came to be

A living thread from Vedic hymns to today's evidence — traditionally dated; the timeline below is the broadly accepted arc.

c. 1500–1000 BCE · The Vedas

Hymns & healing herbs

The Ṛgveda and especially the Atharvaveda hold healing charms and the earliest praises of medicinal plants (oṣadhi). Health, plants and prayer are woven together — the seedbed of a medical tradition.

c. 1000–600 BCE · Āyurveda forms

The science of life

Āyurveda emerges as a systematic body of knowledge — traditionally regarded as an upaveda (auxiliary knowledge) of the Atharvaveda — organising health around balance, digestion and lifestyle.

c. 600 BCE–400 CE · The Great Triad

Charaka, Suśruta, Vāgbhaṭa

The Bṛhat Trayī is composed: the Charaka Saṃhitā (internal medicine), the Suśruta Saṃhitā (surgery & materia medica), later distilled by Vāgbhaṭa in the Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya. These remain the bedrock texts.

c. 700–1600 CE · The Nighaṇṭus

Cataloguing the plants

Materia-medica lexicons such as the Bhāvaprakāśa Nighaṇṭu catalogue each herb's taste, potency and action (dravyaguṇa) — the property-level detail this engine leans on most.

Today · Tradition meets evidence

Honest by design

Modern research and the Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia of India sit alongside the classics. This engine carries every claim with its source and an honesty rating — never overstating what is proven.

First principles

The ideas it all rests on

दोष
Doṣa

Three functional energies — Vāta (movement), Pitta (transformation), Kapha (structure). Wellness is their balance.

अग्नि
Agni

The digestive & metabolic fire. Strong agni means food becomes nourishment, not toxic residue (ama).

धातु
Dhātu

The seven body tissues, each formed from the last — from plasma all the way to reproductive essence.

ओजस्
Ojas

The subtle essence of healthy digestion & tissue — the basis of immunity, stamina and lustre.

रसायन
Rasāyana

Rejuvenation: practices and herbs that restore vitality and slow decline.

द्रव्यगुण
Dravyaguṇa

How a herb acts — its taste (rasa), potency (vīrya) and post-digestive effect (vipāka).

Why you can trust it

Honesty over hype

Every recommendation forms a chain: concern → herb → why → evidence → source. The evidence tier tells you exactly how strong the backing is — per use.

Classical text

Documented in a classical text. Traditional use only — no modern study claimed.

Established tradition

Widespread, long-standing traditional use across lineages and texts.

Preliminary research

Early lab, animal, or small human studies suggest activity — promising, not proven.

Clinical evidence

Supported by one or more controlled human trials (RCTs) for that specific use.
